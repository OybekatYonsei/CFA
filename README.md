# CFA Level 1 Study Program

## Overview
- **Exam Date**: November 11, 2026
- **Total Study Hours Required**: ~280 hours
- **Study Duration**: 28 Weeks (April 28 – Nov 11, 2026)
- **Format**: Computer-Based (CBT)
- **Structure**: 10 Volumes, 3,417 pages

---

## CFA Level 1 Topic Areas & Weights

| Topic | Exam Weight | Study Focus |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Ethical & Professional Standards | 15-20% | High-weight, completely conceptual. |
| Quantitative Methods | 10-12% | Math-heavy, foundation for other subjects. |
| Economics | 8-12% | GSIS specialty—leverage existing knowledge. |
| Financial Statement Analysis (FSA) | 13-17% | Toughest section, longest study time. |
| Corporate Issuers | 8-12% | Governance, working capital, NPV. |
| Equity Investments | 10-12% | Organization, indexes, DDM, valuation. |
| Fixed Income | 10-12% | Pricing, yield, duration, securitization. |
| Derivatives | 5-8% | Forwards, futures, swaps, options. |
| Alternative Investments | 5-8% | Conceptual: Real estate, PE, hedge funds. |
| Portfolio Management | 5-8% | CAPM, Risk/Return, Biases. |

## 28-Week Study Timetable

### **Phase 1: University Semester (Light Pace, 6–8 hrs/week)**
*Goal: Build steady habits without burning out before graduation.*

#### Week 1: Apr 28 – May 3 | Ethics — Foundations (6 hrs)
- [ ] V10 LM1: Ethics & Trust in the Investment Profession (R89)
- [ ] Read each LOS carefully — ethics is conceptual, not math
- **Tip**: Start with Ethics: it's conceptual (no math), high-weight, and builds good study habits during a busy semester.

## Daily Study Tips

### Phase 1 (University Semester)
- **Goal**: 6-8 hrs/week
- Avoid burn out; 1 hr daily on weekdays + 2.5 hrs on Saturday.

### Phase 2 (Post-Graduation)
- **Goal**: 10-12 hrs/week
- Leverage your GSIS degree to breeze through Econ.
- Dedicate longer continuous blocks to FSA and Fixed Income formulas.
